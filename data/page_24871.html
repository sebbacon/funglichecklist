Stereocaulon Hoffm., Deutschl. Fl.: 128 (1796)

Description: Primary Thallus crustose, of basal granules (�phyllocladia�) variously appearing warted, areolate or squamulose; in most species disappearing early. Secondary thallus shrubby, erect, solid-stemmed, often richly branched (�pseudopodetia�), corticate or not, covered in wart-like, coralloid or flattened, squamule-like, corticate phyllocladia which, depending on their distribition, result in the pseudopodetia appearing dorsiventral or not. Cephalodia frequent, containing Nostoc or Stigonema. Photobiont trebouxioid. Medulla lax. Cartilaginous axis of thick-walled, longitudinal hyphae present. Ascomata apothecia, terminal; disc usually becoming convex, pale to dark brown. Thalline exciple absent. True exciple raised to � excluded. Hypothecium mostly colourless. Hamathecium of paraphyses, simple; apices mainly with a brown cap in K/I with a blue outer layer, and a blue apical dome � with a central, darker blue tubular structure, Porpidia-like. Asci 8-spored, clavate to cylindrical. Ascospores colourless, thin-walled, ellipsoid, acicular or fusiform, 1- to 13-septate. Conidiomata pycnidia, mostly immersed at tips of phyllocladia, ovoid to spherical, darkened about ostiole. Conidia simple, thread-like to cylindrical, straight or curved. Chemistry: atranorin (K+ yellow) present in all species, also lobaric and stictic (with � nonstictic) acids, or fatty acids present in certain species. Ecology: mostly on rocks and montane, also occurring on metal-rich spoil heaps, on soil, shingle, amongst mosses on the ground and some near towns on brick..
