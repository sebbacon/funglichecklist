Rinodina (Ach.) Gray, Nat. Arr. Brit. Pl. 1: 448 (1821)

Berengeria Trevis., Rivista Period. Lav. Regia Accad. Sci., Padova: 265 (1853) [1851-1852]

Description: Thallus crustose, thick, thin or disappearing, pale to dark grey, ochraceous or brown, continuous, cracked-areolate, granular, rarely isidiate; prothallus absent, limiting, or entire and dark. Photobiont trebouxioid. Ascomata apothecia, immersed or sessile, usually frequent, continguous or not. Thalline exciple concolorous with the disc or thallus, entire or crenulate, persistent or excluded at maturity, with or without a distinct cortex; cortex when present cellular, occasionally pigmented, sometimes columnar below or structure occluded with pigment, peripheral hyphae usually pigmented. True exciple colourless or rarely brown, prosoplectenchymatous. Disc brown to black, flat or convex at maturity, rarely pruinose. Epithecium brown or rarely blue-black (R. colobina). Hymenium colourless, I+ blue. Hypothecium colourless, rarely brownish or dark brown, stipe not usually present, colourless or dark. Hamathecium of paraphyses, mostly unbranched except near apices, apices expanded, brown-capped. Asci 8-spored, clavate, inner walls uniform or not. Ascospores 1(�3)-septate, mainly double-walled, walls thickened, septum well-developed at maturity, structure often complex, grey-green or pale to dark brown; surface smooth or ornamented (x1000). Conidiomata pycnidia, rarely present. Conidia bacilliform or in one species (R. lecideina) thread-like. Chemistry: depsides + depsidones present in some species, but more chemical investigation needed. Ecology: on a wide variety of substrata..
