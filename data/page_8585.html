Description: Primary thallus (basal) squamulose (subg. Cladonia), or crustose (subg. Cladina) and soon disappearing; basal squamules (subg. Cladonia) closely adpressed or ascending to erect, rounded to elongate, entire or variously indented, occasionally sorediate, often persistent, occasionally dominant. Upper surface usually corticate, the cortex dense, of � vertical hyphae. Lower surface without rhizines, decorticate. Photobiont trebouxioid. Medulla usually 2-layered, the outer white, of � loose hyphae, containing algal glomerules, the inner is glassy or white, cartilaginous, with conglutinate hyphae surrounding central canal. Secondary thallus (erect) consisting of usually hollow podetia, growing from margins or upper surface of primary thallus, blunt, pointed or cup-forming, simple or branched; upper surface corticate (lacking in subg. Cladina and some other species), often with � decorticate areas, corticate granules, soredia, or squamules which may be peeling or not, occasionally areolate; apices, cups and axils closed or perforate. Ascomata apothecia, at apices of podetia, on cup rims or rarely sessile on basal squamules. Thalline exciple absent. True exciple often becoming excluded. Hymenium red, pale or dark brown. Asci elongate clavate, thickened at the apex, 8-spored, with a I+ blue tholus and I+ blue outer gelatinous sheath. Ascospores simple, colourless, fusiform, oblong or ovoid. Conidiomata pycnidia, at apices of podetia, cup rims or on basal squamules, sessile or short-stalked, cylindrical to top-like.
