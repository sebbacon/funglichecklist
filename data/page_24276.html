Mycoblastus Norman, Nytt Mag. Natur. 7: 236 (1853)

Description: Thallus crustose, continuous or granular, sometimes irregularly cracked-areolate, very variable, whitish-grey to grey. Prothallus sometimes present, grey or blackish. Photobiont chlorococcoid, possibly trebouxioid. Soralia often present. Ascomata apothecia, flat to convex or subglobose, sessile or adpressed, black. Thalline exciple absent. True exciple well developed, often excluded at maturity. Epithecium colourless or interspersed with violet granules, K+ blue-green. Hymenium colourless, blue-black or brownish, I+ blue. Hypothecium colourless, pale brown or red. Hamathecium of anastomosing paraphyses, intergrading imperceptibly into the hyphal network of the hypothecium. Asci 1- to 2(�3)-spored, cylindrical-clavate, very thick-walled; apical dome I+ blue, with a distinct ocular chamber (especially when young); outer layer thickened above, forming an I+ dark blue cap over the ascus apex. Ascospores medium to very large; walls strongly thickened, simple, oblong or ellipsoid, colourless, multinucleate. Conidiomata pycnidia, immersed in the thallus, visible as minute black dots; wall green in upper part, K�, N+ reddish. Conidiogenous cells short. Conidia bacilliform, simple, colourless. Chemistry: atranorin; fatty acids, depsides, depsidones and pigments. Ecology: on � acidic substrata, predominantly on bark and wood, more rarely on rocks and associated mosses..
