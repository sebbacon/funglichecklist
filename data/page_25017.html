Umbilicaria Hoffm., Descr. Pl. Cl. Crypt. 1(1): 8 (1789)

Lichen jacquinii J.F. Gmel., Syst. Nat. 2(2): 1374 (1791)
Gyrophora Ach., Method. Lich.: 100 (1803)
Actinogyra Schol., Nytt Mag. Natur. 75: 28 (1934)
Omphalodiscus Schol., Nytt Mag. Natur. 75: 23 (1934)

Description: Thallus foliose, single- or multi-lobed, dorsiventral, attached at a single point by a central or excentric strand (umbilicate; Fig. XX), soft, pliable and somewhat leathery when wet, brittle when dry. Upper surface pale grey-brown to black, smooth to warted-areolate, sometimes folded or reticulate-ridged with a raised central area, margins sinuous, entire or incised, rhizinate or not. Lower surface smooth or warted-areolate, sometimes pitted, rarely with plate-like structures, black or pale brown-pink; rhizines present or absent, not attaching thallus. Isidia and soredia occasionally present. Upper cortex at times overlain by an amorphous zone. Photobiont chlorococcoid. Medulla loose or compact, not always clearly differentiated from lower cortex. Ascomata apothecia, � irregular, immersed, sessile or stalked; disc black, flat or � convex, mostly gyrose (Fig. XX), occasionally smooth with a central, protruding button of sterile tissue. Thalline exciple absent. Asci 8-spored, elongate-clavate, thick-walled, apical dome I+ blue. Ascospores ellipsoid, simple and colourless or muriform, becoming brown. Conidiomata pycnidia, uni- or multi-loculate. Conidiogenous cells in chains, enteroblastic, acrogenous or pleurogenous. Conidia shortly cylindrical. Thallospores present in non-isidiate, non-sorediate, rarely fruiting species, originating from lower cortex or on rhizines, single-celled or of several cells. Chemistry: gyrophoric acid frequent, umbilicaric and norstictic acid occasional..
