Scoliciosporum A. Massal., Ric. auton. lich. crost.: 104 (1852)

Description: Thallus crustose, glaucous green, green, grey-white or brownish black, thin, not corticate, finely granular-scurfy to almost powdery, at times resembling growths of Desmococcus; prothallus absent, Soralia rare. Photobiont chlorococcoid, often forming goniocysts. Ascomata apothecia, at first flat, soon, convex, sessile, constricted at base, whitish, yellowish, pale brown to black. Thalline exciple absent. True exciple evident in young apothecia, soon excluded, of radiating, branched and anastomosing hyphae, pale or colourless. Epithecium colourless, brownish, blue-green or olivaceous, sometimes interspersed with granules. Hymenium colourless, olivaceous, blue-green, brown or almost colourless above and then sometimes surrounded by numerous small granules, merging into exciple; I+ blue. Hypothecium colourless or pale, upper part of erect hyphae, randomly orientated below. Hamathecium of paraphyses, septate, branched and anastomosed in a gelatinous matrix, resembling excipular hyphae; apices not or only slightly thickened, sometimes a few with dark brown caps in young apothecia. Asci 8-spored, broadly clavate, or clavate-cylindrical, Lecanora-type with broad apical cushion. Ascospores acicular, sickle-shaped or bent spirally, rarely elongate-spindle-shaped and attenuated towards one end, straight or curved, colourless, 3- to multi-transverse septate, the septa very thin, often indistinct. Conidiomata pycnidia, spherical, � immersed to emergent, wall dark brown to colourless. Conidia of two types: macroconidia rod-shaped, straight or slightly curved; microconidia curved..
