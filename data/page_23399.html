Description: Thallus crustose, smooth, rimose, areolate, granular, or slightly placodioid, sometimes immersed, whitish to grey, brown or yellowish, often delimited by a dark prothallus and mosaic-forming, corticate or not. Photobiont chlorococcoid. Ascomata apothecia, immersed, emergent or superficial from the beginning, black, sometimes white-pruinose. Thalline exciple absent, or in immersed apothecia not distinguishable from the thallus (cryptolecanorine). True exciple thin, pale and inconspicuous to well-developed and dark-pigmented. Epithecium brown to olive-green, K�, N� or N+ reddish. Hymenium colourless, or greenish in upper part, I+ blue, with or without numerous oil droplets that do not dissolve in K. Hypothecium pale or more usually dark brown, sometimes olivaceous in part. Hamathecium of paraphyses, septate, simple or branched in upper third, apices swollen and pigmented, many with a dark brown cap. Asci (4�)8(�many)-spored, clavate, Lecanora-type. Ascospores brown, 1-septate, rarely 3-septate or submuriform, ellipsoid, oblong or fusiform, straight or curved; wall of uniform thickness, or thickened at the septum (rarely also subapically), sometimes thinner and paler at the apices, surface smooth or finely ornamented (use oil immersion), without a distinct perispore. Conidiomata pycnidia, immersed; wall dark brown at least in upper part. Conidia simple, colourless, ellipsoid to oblong, bacilliform, fusiform-cylindrical, or thread-like and curved. Chemistry: paradepsides, tridepsides, depsidones, xanthones and unidentified substances reported. Ecology: on a wide range of substrata, especially siliceous rocks and acid stonework, bark, wood, soil, bryophytes and lichens. A large, cosmopolitan genus of c. 400 species..
