Calicium Pers., Ann. Bot. (Usteri) 7: 20 (1794)

Phacotrum Gray, Nat. Arr. Brit. Pl. 1: 482 (1821)
Caliciella Vain., Acta Soc. Fauna Flora fenn. 57(no. 1): 52 (1927)

Description: Thallus crustose, verrucose-granular or immersed, grey to grey-grey, pale yellow or dark green. Photobiont Trebouxia. Ascomata apothecia, mostly with a long, distinct stalk and globose to lens-shaped head, rarely sessile; stalk of sclerotized, blackish brown to greenish black, irregularly interwoven hyphae. True exciple well-developed, supporting the dry spore mass. Asci cylindrical to clavate, formed singly from ascogenous hyphae with croziers, dissolving at an early age. Ascospores 1-septate, forming a well-developed, black, dry-spore mass; wall thick, dark brown, often with a distinctive ornamentation. Conidiomata pycnidia, sessile or slightly immersed, simple, globose, ostiole punctiform. Conidiophores branched. Conidiogenous cells subcylindrical, enteroblastic. Conidia broadly ellipsoid to short-cylindrical, colourless, non-septate. Chemistry: ï¿½-orcinol depsidone, xanthones, vulpinic acid and vulpinic acid derivatives present. Ecology: usually on dry bark and wood (C. corynellum (3) occurs on siliceous rocks, in rather humid and sheltered situations..
