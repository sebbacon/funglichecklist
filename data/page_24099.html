Leproloma Nyl. ex Cromb., Monogr. Lich. Brit. 1: 348 (1894)

Description: Thallus leprose, rarely granular, ecorticate, of usually powdery, convex granules or eroded to leave a leprose membrane, often with powdery lobes best developed at the margin of the thallus; pale, often tinged yellow, sometimes grey; lobes (when present) and granules covered by loosely entangled hyphae, which often project outwards (x 25). Medulla white. Hypothallus primitive, whitish-grey to brownish, of a continuous weft of loosely entangled, branched hyphae, these usually thick and brownish, in contrast to the narrow, colourless hyphae of the thallus. Photobiont species of Chlorophyta. Ascomata apothecia, very rare, small, superficial, orbicular, urceolate. Thalline exciple, tumid, sorediate, concolorous with the thallus. Discs brownish. Hymenium short, colourless. Hypothecium brown. Hamathecium of paraphyses straight, simple, septate, the cells slightly constricted at the septa, the tips slightly swollen. Asci and Ascospores unknown. Conidiomata unknown. Chemistry: dibenzofurans, atranorin and roccellic acid..
