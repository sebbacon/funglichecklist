Polyblastia A. Massal., Ric. auton. lich. crost.: 147 (1852)

Sporodictyon A. Massal., Flora 35: 326 (1852)

Description: Thallus crustose, immersed or superficial, often inconspicuous, smooth to uneven, rarely subsquamulose. Soredia rarely present (see 15). Photobiont chlorococcoid. Ascomata perithecia, superficial or immersed in thalline warts, forming pits in limestone. Involucrellum present or absent. Thalline exciple in a few species. True exciple completely black and friable, or pale below. Hamathecium of periphyses; paraphyses absent at maturity. Asci (1�)2- or 8-spored, saccate, Verrucaria-type, thin-walled. Ascospores muriform or submuriform, � ellipsoid or ovate, rarely reniform, colourless to deep brown, sometimes with a gelatinous perispore. Conidiomata pycnidia, rare, immersed. Conidia bacilliform, produced terminally from short conidiogenous cells. Chemistry: lichen products not detected. Ecology: on limestones, mica schist, epidiorite, chalk pebbles, mortar, brick, on soil, overgrowing mosses, on siliceous rocks in and by streams and lakes, rarely on bark..
